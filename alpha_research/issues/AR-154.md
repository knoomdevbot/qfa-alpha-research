## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Public SEC Form 144 notices of proposed affiliate/control-person sales, traded only after EDGAR acceptance/public timestamp plus a conservative next-session lag, may identify near-term supply pressure or subsequent relief/reversal in liquid U.S. common stocks.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Form 144 is a structured public notice of intended sale size/timing by affiliates or holders of restricted/control securities. Large proposed sales relative to shares outstanding or issuer liquidity may create short-horizon supply pressure, while stale/absorbed notices may revert.
- Expected failure mode: Form 144 notices are already anticipated through 10b5-1 plans, RSU/option programs, broker execution, or later Form 4 sale reporting; signal collapses into generic SEC filing attention, issuer momentum/reversal, size/liquidity, sector beta, or price/volume pressure.
- Falsifier: Reject before performance if a reproducible timestamp-safe Form 144 event table with parsed proposed-sale fields, conservative liquid common-stock issuer mapping, duplicate/amendment clustering, and breadth/concentration gates cannot be built. Reject after performance if shifted/shuffled labels, Form 4 sale controls, generic SEC filing-day controls, momentum/reversal, sector/beta, price/volume pressure, size/liquidity, or sale-size placebo controls match or dominate the primary signal.

- Risk constraints: Trade only after EDGAR acceptance/public timestamp mapped to next liquid session; never trade on approximate sale date before public filing; exclude ambiguous security classes and mappings; cluster same issuer/filer/broker/approximate-sale-date notices.
