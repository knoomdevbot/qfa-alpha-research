## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped official FDA approval decisions for U.S.-listed biotech/pharma issuers may create post-decision drift or reversal in liquid common stocks, but only if an official FDA event table can map decision dates to tradable issuers without price/volume inference or company-PR hindsight.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: FDA approval decisions are discrete regulatory information shocks that can reprice product pipelines, financing capacity, and takeover/partnering odds. The intended edge is regulatory decision timing, not generic biotech momentum or SEC filing attention.
- Expected failure mode: Official FDA sources provide dates but not tradable first-public timestamps; sponsor names map to subsidiaries/private/acquired/non-U.S. entities; mapped liquid events fall below breadth gates; events concentrate in large pharma/generic sponsors; XBI/IBB beta, matched non-event biotech days, momentum/reversal, or shifted-label controls explain any apparent effect.

Exclude ADRs, funds, preferreds, warrants, private companies, acquired entities without conservative event-date mapping, and ambiguous sponsors.
- Product-selection filters: Conservative sponsor-to-issuer/ticker mapping at event date; event has official FDA decision date or official FDA first-public page date; issuer has qfa/Alpaca daily bars around the next regular session; dropped-event logs for subsidiaries, generics, non-U.S. sponsors, stale tickers, and ambiguous mappings.
- Selected universe: To be fixed after source scout. Performance backtest is prohibited until the source gate builds a timestamp-safe event table with >=150 conservatively mapped liquid FDA approval-decision events after deduplication and concentration checks.

- Data source: Official FDA Drugs@FDA downloadable data files, CDER approval/letter/action-package pages, and official FDA press announcements where available. openFDA may be used only as a supplemental discovery aid, not as the sole point-in-time source. Company PRs and SEC filings may be used only for supplemental mapping/cross-checks and must be labeled non-primary.
- Signal features: Event indicator for official FDA approval decisions. Complete response letters or rejection events are optional/secondary only if official FDA first-public timestamp/date is independently proven; otherwise exclude them from the primary event table. Trade no earlier than the next regular session after conservative public availability.

- Position sizing: Zero-weight scaffold for source-gate phase. Any later performance test must use predeclared equal-weight event portfolios with liquidity caps and no performance-based issuer selection.
- Risk constraints: No future data; next-session timing only; manual precision audit on a stratified sample; issuer/sponsor/year/therapeutic-area concentration caps; reject if event table breadth, mapping precision, or timestamp safety fails.
