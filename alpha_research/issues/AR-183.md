## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamp-safe first-vintage surprises and revisions in official Census/HUD New Residential Construction releases — especially housing starts and building permits, with completions as a diagnostic — may forecast short-horizon allocation across homebuilder, REIT, materials, banks, rates, credit, and broad equity ETFs.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Housing construction is rate-sensitive and cyclically important. Permits and starts update expectations for residential investment, materials demand, mortgage/credit exposure, homebuilder earnings, REIT/rates sensitivity, and broader risk appetite. Revisions may matter because Census releases explicitly revise prior months.
- Expected failure mode: The signal is a well-known monthly macro release with limited sample size; effects may be instantaneous/intraday and unavailable to daily bars; static beta, SPY/equal-weight, rates trend, ETF TSMOM/reversal, CPI/PPI/labor/FOMC controls, shifted/random labels, inverted shocks, or one regime may dominate.
