## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamp-safe monthly Federal Reserve G.19 consumer-credit impulses, especially acceleration/deceleration in revolving credit growth relative to nonrevolving/total credit, may forecast next-month consumer-risk appetite and rotations among consumer discretionary/staples, credit, equity beta, rates, and financial ETFs after the official release date.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Revolving credit growth can proxy household willingness or need to borrow and changing consumer-risk appetite. A deceleration or contraction may indicate consumer stress and tighter discretionary spending, while acceleration relative to nonrevolving credit may indicate risk appetite or late-cycle leverage. The signal is potentially distinct from bank-balance-sheet H.8, Fed liquidity H.4.1, and FINRA margin-debt leverage-cycle releases.
- Expected failure mode: Monthly sample is sparse; signal may be revised/backfilled, stale, procyclical, dominated by SPY/equal-weight/static sleeves, ETF TSMOM/reversal, credit-spread or consumer-sector momentum, shifted labels, inverted labels, H.8/H.4.1/FINRA margin-debt proxies, or one crisis regime.

- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca daily-bar availability, liquid broad ETF exposure, sufficient overlap with post-release G.19 history, fixed ex-ante inclusion by economic exposure, no return-based universe selection, and drop short-history/unavailable symbols before evaluation.
- Selected universe: To be fixed after source/vintage gate; expected starting set XLY, XLP, HYG, LQD, SPY, RSP, IWM, TLT, SHY, XLF subject to coverage checks.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: The pool maps to household-credit and consumer-risk channels while including credit/rates/equity controls. Monthly observations are sparse and HYG starts later than broad ETFs, so tests must avoid feature mining and overfitting.
- Data source: Official Federal Reserve G.19 dated release pages and releaseDates archive; optional FRED/ALFRED diagnostics only if vintage-safe. qfa/Alpaca real daily ETF bars only after the source/vintage gate passes; no CSV-backed market data; no daemon; no orders.
- Signal features: Predeclared monthly changes/z-scores in revolving consumer credit growth, nonrevolving credit growth, total consumer credit growth, revolving-minus-nonrevolving impulse, and annualized flow/outstanding fields parsed directly from dated G.19 tables.
- Excluded features: Delinquency and charge-off data are excluded from the primary hypothesis unless a separate official timestamp-safe source/vintage gate is opened for the Fed Charge-Off and Delinquency release.

Official Federal Reserve G.19 `releaseDates.json` was reachable with 361 dated releases from 1996-06-11 through 2026-06-05, and sampled dated release pages contained consumer-credit tables for revolving, nonrevolving, total credit, annualized changes, flows, and outstanding balances. Queue only as a strict source/vintage-first fast-falsification scout with severe controls and no CSV-backed market data.
