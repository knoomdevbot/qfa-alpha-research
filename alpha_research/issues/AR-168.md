## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Public daily OHLCV volatility-ETP term-state proxies may contain enough information about volatility-risk appetite to allocate next-week risk/defensive ETF exposure after costs and hostile controls.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Relative behavior among short- and medium-term volatility ETPs and inverse vol exposure can proxy volatility-market stress/normalization and risk-premium demand, which may spill into equities, credit, duration, and gold.
- Expected failure mode: The proxy is just VIXY/TLT stress beta, ETF TSMOM, realized-vol carry, or SPY/equal-weight exposure; available ETP history is short/survivorship-biased; the result is redundant with rejected AR-052/065/066/111-style volatility allocator families.

## Implementation
- Candidate product pool: Public volatility ETP proxies VIXY, VXX, VXZ, SVXY plus broad liquid ETFs across equities/style/sectors, credit, duration, gold, commodities, and USD proxy.
- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca daily coverage, sufficient common history, liquid broad ETFs, non-levered/non-inverse primary allocation universe except volatility ETP inputs, no performance-ranked selection.
- Selected universe: volatility inputs VIXY, VXX, VXZ, SVXY; risk sleeve SPY, QQQ, IWM, EFA, EEM, XLF, XLE, XLK, HYG, LQD, DBC; defensive sleeve TLT, IEF, GLD, UUP, SHY.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: This is a public daily OHLCV vol-ETP term-state proxy, not true implied volatility, VIX futures curve, options, or variance-risk-premium data. Common history begins only 2018-01-18 because VXX/VXZ have shorter available current-product history, so ETP history is short and survivorship/product-definition biased.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only; no CSV market data; no daemon; no orders.
- Signal features: Lagged close-based 20-day changes in VIXY/VXZ ratio, VIXY, VXZ, VXX, and SVXY; weekly rebalance, weights shifted one trading day.
- Model form: Fast-falsification validator / rule-based allocator.
- Parameters / search space: Predeclared weekly rebalance and lagged features; no post-hoc selection of volatility ETPs or risk sleeves by performance.
- Position sizing: Equal-weight risk sleeve when vol term-state is risk-on; otherwise fixed defensive sleeve TLT/IEF/GLD/UUP/SHY.
- Risk constraints: 5/10/20 bps one-way cost haircuts; max relevant correlation <= 0.60 hard; reject if simple VIXY/TLT/stress-beta or TSMOM controls dominate.

## Evaluation
- Random-period protocol: 60 random windows over common history with 1/2/3-year lengths, plus named stress windows.
- Costs/slippage assumptions: 5/10/20 bps one-way turnover costs; 10 bps primary gate; 20 bps must not fully collapse.
- Orthogonality checks vs alpha library: Reconstructable SPY, equal-weight ETF, TLT, VIXY/TLT stress, and ETF TSMOM controls were used; prior accepted/watchlist daily return paths were not retained, so broader library orthogonality is approximated.
- Acceptance threshold: 10 bps median random-window Sharpe > 0, p25 non-hostile, positive-window rate >= 55%, 20 bps not collapsed, max relevant corr <= 0.60, and full model beats simple VIXY/TLT/stress-beta and TSMOM controls.

## Decision
Rejected. The full-period and median random-window result is mildly positive, but it fails core hostile gates: p25 random-window Sharpe is negative/hostile, SPY and equal-weight controls dominate on Sharpe, and the correlation to a simple VIXY/TLT stress-beta allocator breaches the hard 0.60 cap. The evidence supports the falsifier that this rule is mostly a stress-beta allocator, not a robust orthogonal Vol-ETP term-state alpha.
