## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Timestamped issuer share-repurchase authorization or increase announcements may create post-announcement drift or liquidity support in liquid U.S. common stocks, but only if an official SEC event table can identify actual new/additional/expanded/renewed board authorizations with public timestamps, conservative issuer mapping, and sufficient event breadth.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: New or enlarged buyback authorization can signal management confidence, reduce expected float supply, and create future demand support. The potential edge is in the announced authorization event, not in routine quarterly repurchase activity or remaining-capacity boilerplate.
- Expected failure mode: Parser cannot separate true authorization actions from ordinary buyback disclosures; conservative common-stock mapping collapses breadth; events concentrate in mega-cap earnings press releases; generic filing-day attention, earnings-release drift, momentum/reversal, buyback seasonality, size/liquidity, or matched non-event issuer filings explain any apparent effect.
