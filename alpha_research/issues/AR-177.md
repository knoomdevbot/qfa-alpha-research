## Alpha Hypothesis
- Claim: Within broad liquid U.S. common stocks, temporary idiosyncratic residual dislocations relative to mechanically formed peer clusters may partially converge over the next 1-5 sessions after controlling for market, size/growth, sector-ETF beta, single-name reversal/momentum, and liquidity-reversal proxies.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: Investors often trade baskets and factor/sector exposures faster than they reprice stock-specific residuals within economically similar peer groups. A daily close-to-close residual-spread signal can test whether extreme lagged residual outliers mean-revert within locally correlated peers without relying on filings, fundamentals, earnings timestamps, intraday data, or unavailable borrow/order mechanics.
- Expected failure mode: The signal is only generic single-name reversal, close-volume/liquidity reversal, sector ETF beta reversal, low-vol/quality, mega-cap concentration, or a noisy pairs/stat-arb clone; turnover and 10-20 bps costs erase any edge; mechanically formed clusters are unstable or dominated by one sector/regime.

## Implementation Plan
- Candidate product pool: Broad U.S.-listed liquid common stocks available through qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV. Start from a large ex-ante tradable-common-stock universe if the evaluator has one; otherwise construct from Alpaca/qfa tradable active assets before performance scoring. Target 250-800 names after coverage/liquidity filters. ETF signals/controls may include SPY, QQQ, IWM, sector SPDR ETFs, and cash proxy SHY, but the primary traded sleeve is common stocks only.
- Product-selection filters: qfa/Alpaca daily-bar availability; active/tradable common-stock-like assets; minimum price floor; minimum median dollar-volume/volume; sufficient history for 252-day warmup and random windows; missing-bar/corporate-action sanity; exclude ETFs/funds/preferreds/closed-end funds/ADRs if classification is ambiguous; no return-performance-ranked symbol selection. Record selected/dropped counts and reasons.
- Selected universe: To be fixed by evaluator before performance review from coverage/liquidity/classification filters. Peer clusters are formed only from lagged return/correlation/beta information inside each training window or from predeclared rolling lookbacks; no future full-sample clustering.
- Universe-selection rationale / limitations: Broad common-stock breadth is required to avoid the narrow AR-005 mega-cap-tech pair limitation and to test whether residual convergence generalizes. Mechanical OHLCV clustering is intentionally source-safe but only approximates true economic peers; if selected breadth is too small or clusters are unstable, reject rather than force results.
- Data source: qfa/Alpaca real daily OHLCV only; no CSV market data; no `--data-csv`; no daemon; no orders; no new external source/vintage dependency.
- Signal features: Lagged daily close-to-close returns; rolling beta/residuals versus SPY, QQQ/IWM, and sector ETF proxies where available; rolling correlation-cluster assignment; peer-cluster residual mean and dispersion; z-scored residual deviation from peer mean; optional volume/liquidity sanity filter only as a control/diagnostic, not as primary signal; all features shifted at least one trading day.
- Model form: Interpretable rule-based cross-sectional residual-convergence ranker. At each rebalance, long low residual outliers and short/high underweight high residual outliers within clusters with enough members; report long-short primary and long-only/cash diagnostic if shorting is infeasible.
- Parameters / search space: Predeclare tight diagnostics: residual lookback 60/126/252 days; cluster refresh monthly/quarterly using only trailing data; minimum cluster size 8/12; top-bottom quantile 10%/20%; holding period 1/3/5 days; volatility scaling on/off; sector/cluster caps; cost bps 5/10/20. Do not mine arbitrary cluster counts or symbol subsets by performance.
- Position sizing: Equal-risk or equal-weight residual baskets with gross exposure normalized, per-symbol caps, cluster/sector caps, cash/no-trade when breadth or dispersion is insufficient. Keep beta and broad factor exposure diagnostics near neutral for the long-short primary.
- Risk constraints: Strict lagging of clustering and residual estimates; no future membership or full-sample correlation leakage; primary cost gate at 10 bps one-way with 20 bps stress; reject if turnover is excessive, if borrow/short constraints make long-short unrealistic without a viable long-only diagnostic, or if one sector/cluster/year/symbol group dominates.
