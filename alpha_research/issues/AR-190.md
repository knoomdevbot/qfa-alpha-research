## Alpha Hypothesis

- Claim: Official Treasury auction result fields such as bid-to-cover, tendered/accepted amounts, and indirect/direct/primary-dealer participation may contain duration-demand information distinct from deterministic auction-calendar effects.
- Economic / behavioral intuition: unexpectedly strong or weak auction demand can shift duration risk appetite and term-premium perception after official result publication.
- Expected failure mode: Treasury FiscalData/result files lack durable publication timestamps/vintages, result fields are known/anticipated, daily ETF bars are too coarse, or performance is dominated by auction calendar, Treasury payment, FOMC/rate, ETF momentum/reversal, static duration beta, or shifted/random-label controls.
